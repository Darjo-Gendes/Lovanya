# Journal — the emotional archive

**She opens the Journal because…** she wants to remember a look she loved —
or find one she needs again.

**Three-second feeling:** nostalgia and pride. This is her style story, not a
log. **Design memories before databases:** Journal is an emotional archive;
Wardrobe is an organizational tool. They must never feel interchangeable.

**Two modes, two moods:**
- **Feed** = reliving. Day-grouped timeline; the newest look is a HERO card
  (large photography, ribbon badge, serif title + quiet date, soft meta).
  Older looks step down to compact rows — hero/standard/compact rhythm.
- **Library** = boutique browsing, not file management. Search reads like a
  concierge ("Search looks, occasions, colors…"). Facets are pictorial cards
  (wine glass = Occasion, swatch book = Colors, award = Ribboned) that render
  ONLY when they have content — progressive disclosure is law; a new user
  sees a clean feed, never nine empty shelves.

**Design outfits, not cards:** every saved look is a moment — photography
first, rich overlays, elegant type, meaningful metadata ("9 garments ·
Casual day"), never a data row.

**Ribbon, singular:** the ribbon ("memorable moment") is the ONLY save
affordance on looks. Garment hearts belong to the closet. Copy: "Marked as
memorable" — never "bookmarked".

**Look sheet:** reopening a look replays the verdict as a keepsake — headline
first (a compliment), score in the rosewood→gold badge, meters, "What
worked", one gentle thought. Feelings before numbers.

**Cut by decision (do not reintroduce without the evidence gate):** style/mood
labels, indoor/outdoor tags, places search. AI Collections and per-look
extracted-item grids arrive with the Phase-2 pipeline.

**Empty state:** "Your journal starts with one look ♡" + one CTA to Style Me.
Warm, zero guilt.
