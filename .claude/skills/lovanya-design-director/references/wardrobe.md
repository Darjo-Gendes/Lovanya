# Wardrobe — her boutique, not a spreadsheet

**She opens the Wardrobe because…** she wants to see what she owns the way a
boutique would show it — and add new pieces effortlessly.

**Three-second feeling:** abundance and care ("183 pieces thoughtfully
collected ✦"). **Primary action:** Organize Closet (add pieces). Browsing is
the pleasure; adding is the action.

**Hero:** the stats card — but it's a *care* panel, not analytics: Total /
Most Worn / Least Used / Closet Health with photos and warm phrasing ("Well
Balanced — great mix of styles and colors"). Least-used is an invitation
("give it a moment"), never a guilt metric.

**Grid law:** 3-column photo grid; every item shows photo (or tinted art
fallback), name, category, color dot, wear note ("Worn 3× / Not worn yet").
Filter chips scroll horizontally, selected = rosewood gradient. Sort cycles
(recent / worn / A–Z) — no dropdown bureaucracy.

**Dedup is a courtesy, never a merge:** "Looks like your White Linen Shirt —
same piece?" with side-by-side photos. One tap either way. Never silently
merge, never nag.

**Garment hearts live here** (love feeds her preference learning) — distinct
from the Journal's ribbon. Heart = "this piece is me". Item detail sheet:
photo left, serif name, wear count, colors; actions = heart + "Add to
Today's Look".

**Thumbnail future (Phase 2):** catalog-grade product shots arrive from the
pipeline (SAM2 cutout → SDXL render). Until then ItemThumb's photo→art→swatch
fallback chain is the standard — never show a broken or empty frame.
